# Thoth Market Audit

Date: 2026-06-22

Thoth is positioned as a compact XCOM-lite tactics game in LOVE/Lua: six auditors enter a hostile Buried Archive, read enemy intent, use AP/cover/LoS/flanks/overwatch/fog, and solve deterministic institutional-horror missions.

## Position

- Core promise: Into the Breach clarity plus XCOM-style squad identity, cover, LoS, overwatch, evacuation, and class tools.
- Tactical rule: RNG can shape the board before deployment, but board-loaded actions resolve through previewable deterministic rules.
- Vertical slice: Buried Archive only, six-unit squad, six ordered mission variants, fixed-seed procgen validator, no broad campaign promise yet.
- Tone: anomalous bureaucracy, archive containment, debt, audit pressure, sealed records, and hostile institutions.

## Target Audience

- Primary: players who want compact tactical puzzles with readable enemy actions, but also want XCOM-like squad roles, AP choices, cover, flanks, LoS, and overwatch.
- Secondary: Tactical Breach Wizards and Invisible Inc players who value small teams, precise movement, unique ability kits, procedural pressure, and low ambiguity.
- Tone fit: Control and Lobotomy Corporation fans who respond to secret agencies, containment work, hostile offices, anomalous artifacts, and institutional dread.
- Poor fit: players primarily seeking dice accuracy, large strategic management layers, real-time action horror, or long-form character stat progression in this slice.

## Competitive Set

| Reference | Market signal | Thoth adjacency | Thoth separation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Into the Breach | Compact turn-based battles with telegraphed attacks and randomly generated challenges. | Deterministic enemy intent, objective pressure, small boards, visible consequences. | Six-person squad, fog/LoS/cover/overwatch, occult bureaucracy instead of mech defense. |
| XCOM 2 | Tactical layer exposes health, AP, cover, overwatch, concealment, effects, actions, and procedural tactical maps. | AP economy, cover reads, LoS, flanks, overwatch, evac/objective pressure, squad identity. | No hit-percent drama after board load; no promise of a full strategy layer in this slice. |
| XCOM: Chimera Squad | Discrete encounters, breach-and-clear framing, unique agents, teamwork, and combo actions. | Smaller scoped missions, named class kits, breach-like entry pressure, readable team combos. | Thoth is deterministic and horror-forward, not police procedural or urban peacekeeping fiction. |
| Invisible Inc | Stealth, precision, teamwork, and generated high-risk missions. | Procedural validation, hidden/revealed information, costly movement, extraction pressure. | Thoth resolves as open tactical board combat, not stealth-only infiltration. |
| Tactical Breach Wizards | Story-led small-squad tactics with unique abilities and rewinds for experimentation. | Readable ability kits, room-scale tactics, playful tactical verb combos. | Thoth is colder, harsher, and validator-led; rewind is QA/replay infrastructure, not the player promise. |
| Gears Tactics | Fast-paced turn-based squad battles, upgrades, equipment, and large boss encounters. | Class identity, boss phases, cover pressure, equipment/loadout decisions. | Smaller production scope, deterministic previews, less action-power-fantasy tone. |
| Control | Secret agency invaded by an otherworldly threat inside an unpredictable institutional world. | Bureaucratic horror, anomalous spaces, hostile organization logic. | Thoth is a grid tactics game, not third-person action-adventure. |
| Lobotomy Corporation | SCP/Warehouse 13-style monster-management dread inside a facility. | Containment labor, institution-as-threat, anomaly documentation, escalating operational failure. | Thoth is mission tactics with a squad, not facility management simulation. |

## Storefront Angle

- One-line pitch: `Deterministic XCOM-lite tactics in a cursed archive: six auditors read intent, bend cover, and survive institutional horror without hit-roll RNG.`
- Screenshot priority: fog-of-war reveal, overwatch cone, intent legend, six-unit AP HUD, objective pressure, and tile inspector.
- Do not sell the game as a giant tactics sandbox yet. The honest slice is a tight Buried Archive route with strong replay proof.

## Product Risks

- The competitive promise is clarity. Any hidden math, unclear cover/flank state, or unreadable intent breaks the pitch.
- Six units can become UI-heavy. The HUD must keep AP, role, intent risk, and objective state readable at 1280x720.
- Institutional horror needs specificity. Generic dark fantasy language weakens the audience signal.
- The validator is now part of the market claim: generated boards need fixed-seed evidence, reject reasons, and replay determinism before storefront use.
